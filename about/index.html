What’s with the name?

“What is a layman programmer?” you ask? Well, that’s me - or at least what I feel is a suitable description of myself. I know, humble brag. “Care to elaborate on that?” Sure. I graduated from the University of Connecticut in 2012 with a BA in Actuarial Science.

If you’re familiar with Actuarial Science, that’s great - you would have saved me a slightly tedious, several minute long explanation in a face-to-face conversation. If not, don’t worry, you’re not alone. Just throwing out a rough estimate, I’d say that maybe 1 in 3 people know what an actuary actually does (if you’ll pardon the alliteration…) - and by know I mean something besides watching a Ben Stiller movie. Besides, a perfect segue to my next humble brag, right? I’ll resist the urge just this once, and direct you to the Wiki page linked above for more details.

“So getting back to your imaginary my question…” Right, sorry. Getting back to the point, my academic background is in mathematics, not computer science. In fact, up until my final semester of college I didn’t know a lick of programming, as my only prior encounter with code was the Visual Basic course I took in 9th grade. Of course, the little bit I might have learned in that class had long since faded - my sole takeaway being that some instructors take variable naming conventions very seriously way too seriously for a freshman highschool course. But in the Spring semester of my senior year I took a great course on Actuarial Statistics to fulfill a VEE requirement - and was fortunate to have a fantastic professor whose curriculum involved a lot of hands-on work with the R programming language.
